# Commonplace

**Applied integration for senior leaders designing the next generation of organisations.**

Commonplace Ventures is a working studio in the tradition of the **commonplace book** — a scholar's notebook for collecting and integrating knowledge across many domains. What that tradition was to a Renaissance polymath, this practice is to a modern senior leader: an accumulated stock of observation, framework, and judgment, brought fresh to the problem at hand.

Two decades of observation across tech leadership, enterprise advisory, product and platform work, and organisation design — now brought into service of leaders rethinking the shape of their companies in the age of intelligence.

---

## What this is

Most practices choose a lane: strategy or execution, thinking or doing. Commonplace Ventures sits at the intersection — because the gap between insight and implementation is where most organisations lose most of their value.

**Think side** — the judgment to know what your organisation needs to become. Diagnosis, strategy, frameworks, writing. Accumulated observation across two decades of cross-domain work, synthesised into something you can act on.

**Build side** — the capability to help you build it. Hands-on delivery alongside your teams. AI-era products, operating models, agentic workflows. We design it and we build it.

## Who it's for

Senior operators — COO, CEO, CTPO, CTO, Head of Product Engineering — at companies that have moved past "should we use AI" and are now sitting with the harder question: *what does our organisation need to become, and who is going to help us build it?*

## How it engages

1. **Diagnosis** — named problem, COM-B-grade analysis, written report. 2 weeks, fixed fee.
2. **Advisory Retainer** — senior thinking partner across idea and delivery. £10-15K/month.
3. **Build Engagement** — hands-on delivery of a specific AI-era capability. Project-scoped.
4. **Intensive** — focused session with a senior team. Day rate.
5. **Writing** — essays, field notes, pattern library. Public.
