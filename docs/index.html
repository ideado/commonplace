Commonplace Ventures brings together two things that are usually sold separately: the judgment to know what your organisation needs to become, and the capability to help you build it. The work

Most practices choose a lane: strategy or execution, thinking or doing, advisory or delivery. Commonplace Ventures sits at the intersection — because the gap between insight and implementation is where most organisations lose the most value.

We work across two modes, often in the same engagement:

Think — the idea and strategy side

Diagnosis of where your organisation is misshapen for the intelligence era. Naming the specific problem. Understanding the behavioural and structural mechanisms that are holding it in place. Writing the report that gives your senior team a shared picture of what needs to change and why.

This is the work of integrated judgment — accumulated observation across two decades, applied to your specific situation.

Build — the delivery and do side

Turning the diagnosis into motion. Designing the new operating model. Building the AI-era products and capabilities that your organisation needs. Hands-on delivery alongside your teams — not steering committees, but actual work.

This is where the thinking becomes real: prototypes, platforms, changed ways of working, capabilities your teams can own.

The two modes reinforce each other. Strategy without delivery stays on slides. Delivery without strategy builds the wrong things. Commonplace Ventures brings both — as a thinking partner, a diagnostic practitioner, and a hands-on builder.

About

The name

A commonplace book is a scholar's private notebook — not a diary, but a collection. Passages from books. Observations from the field. Frameworks half-formed, arguments not yet finished. Organised by theme so they can be retrieved, connected, and composed from.

Montaigne kept one. Darwin kept one. John Locke designed an indexing system for his. For centuries, it was how a serious mind turned raw exposure into working judgment.

That is what this practice aims to be — for the AI era.

The gap we fill

The world has no shortage of AI content — frameworks, predictions, playbooks, benchmarks, manifestos. What it has a shortage of is integrated judgment.

The kind that comes from two decades of cross-domain observation, synthesised into something a senior leader can actually use on Monday morning. Drawing on:

  • Organisation design and operating model thinking
  • Behaviour change science (COM-B / BCW)
  • Systems thinking and platform economics
  • The emerging science of agentic systems
  • Twenty years of watching what actually happens when large organisations try to change

AI gives access to information. A commonplace gives judgment across time — a claim no AI can yet make on its own behalf.
