# Content Interview Workflow

A structured process for turning field experience into publishable essays — one interview session, one article.

## The bottleneck this solves

The ideas exist. They're built from 25 years of doing the work. The bottleneck is extraction — getting thinking out in a form that can become writing. This workflow structures that as a focused conversation, then turns it into a draft.

## How to start a session

Say: **"Let's do the interview"** or **"Interview me on [topic]"** or give a GitHub issue number.

Claude will:
1. Pull the issue from the content backlog (https://github.com/ideado/commonplace/issues)
2. Present the topic brief and questions
3. Run the interview
4. Synthesise and draft

---

## The interview structure (30–45 min)

### Opening (2 min)
State the topic and the one question we're trying to answer by the end.

### Questions (25–35 min)
3–4 prepared questions, one at a time. After each answer:
- Reflect back the sharpest thing heard (1 sentence)
- One follow-up if something is underdeveloped
- Capture the most precise, quotable phrase

**Good follow-ups:**
- "Can you give me a specific example of that?"
- "What does that look like in practice — what would I actually see?"
- "What's the version of this that most people get wrong?"
- "If you had to put that in one sentence, what would it be?"
- "Is that always true, or are there cases where it flips?"

### Close (3 min)
"If someone reads this and remembers one thing, what should it be?"

---

## After the interview

Claude produces:
1. **3–5 key insights** — the most precise things that came out
2. **One-paragraph summary** — what the essay is actually about
3. **Proposed structure** — hook, 3–4 sections, close

Ritin approves the structure → Claude writes the full draft.

---

## Writing style

From the brand identity:
- Lead with observation, not conclusion
- Specific over abstract — name the thing, not the category
- Declarative sentences. No hedging.
- Pair concrete with philosophical
- Leave room for the reader's judgment — don't over-close
- No jargon. No "game-changer", "unleash", "10x"
- Target: 1,000–1,500 words

---

## Content backlog

Managed as GitHub Issues: https://github.com/ideado/commonplace/issues

Labels:
- `interview-needed` — ready to interview, questions prepared
- `draft` — interview done, writing in progress
- `ready-to-publish` — draft approved, waiting to go live

**Cadence target:** one session → one published piece. One per day to start.
